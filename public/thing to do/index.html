Among the historic monuments, the Red Fort is also one of the most significant monument in the heart of Delhi city, India. The red fort was established as the castle fort of powerful sovereign of ancient India in the city of Delhi. The red fort is enclosed by a rubble stone wall, with bastions, gates and wickets at intervals. The design of the stunning palace is founded on Islamic samples, but each construction discloses architectural basics typical of Mughal erection. The Red Fort is an unpredictable octagon, with two long sides on the east and west, and with two fundamental entrances, one on the west and the other on the south, called Lahori and Delhi gates separately. While the dividers, entryways and a couple of different structures in the fortification are built of red sandstone, marble has been to a great extent utilized as a part of the castles.
